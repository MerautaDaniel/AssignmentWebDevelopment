Some history

Widespread since Roman times, lavender has had different names over time. The ancient Greeks called him Nardus, and the Romans called him Lavandarius. From the beginning of Christendom, lavender oil was used as a holy oil, being considered to have healing properties, Although it was a widespread plant in antiquity, the fact that the Greeks called it Nardus or Nard, a name derived from the ancient Syrian city of Naarda, is believed to have been one of the areas of origin of the first lavender species. Now, lavender is found all over the world, from the Canary Islands, throughout Europe, in North and Northeast Africa, to Asia, India and the United States. Modern studies have proven the calming effects of lavender and lavender is scientifically recognized for its medicinal properties, so it is not surprising that ancient civilizations knew its benefits. Lavender is good for insomnia, anxiety and depression, among many others.
